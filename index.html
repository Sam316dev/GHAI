Our Constitution

Established to guide our programs and governance, the GHAI Constitution ensures equitable access to accurate health information across Africa through a transparent, democratic framework.

Our charter binds our General Congress and Executive Committee to strict operational accountability, requiring performance-based tenures and dual-signatory management of all organizational funds solely for community health interventions.

Constitution Highlights

Our Purpose (Art. II & III)

GHAI exists to build a healthier Africa by closing the gaps in health awareness, access, and preventive care. Our mission is simple: educate, empower, and engage communities with life-saving health information that promotes early detection and healthy living.

What We Do (Art. IV)

  • Raise awareness on preventable diseases — hypertension, diabetes, HIV/AIDS, and mental health disorders.
  • Organize outreaches — community health workshops, free clinics, and digital health campaigns.
  • Distribute resources in clear, culturally relevant local languages so no one is left out.
  • Champion youth — supporting youth-led innovations and volunteerism in public health.
  • Build partnerships that advance health equity across the continent.

Who Leads Us (Art. VI)

GHAI is governed by a General Congress — our highest decision-making body — and an Executive Committee that handles day-to-day operations. Officers serve two-year terms renewable by performance, ensuring only committed people hold leadership positions.

Financial Integrity (Art. IX & X)

All funds are held in an official bank account under the GHAI name and require any two of three designated signatories to authorize a transaction. Every naira raised — through donations, grants, sponsorships, or fundraising events — goes solely toward our community health objectives. The Treasurer presents financial reports at every General Meeting.

Your Voice Matters (Art. XI)

This constitution can only be changed by a two-thirds majority vote of members with 14 days' notice — ensuring no single person can alter the direction of GHAI without broad community agreement.
